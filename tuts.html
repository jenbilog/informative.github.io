Basic Chords

C Major

1. Place your fingers: Put your ring finger on the third fret of the A string, middle finger on the second fret of the D string, and index finger on the first fret of the B string.
2. Strum the strings: Strum from the A string down to the high E string, avoiding the low E string.

G Major

1. Place your fingers: Put your ring finger on the third fret of the low E string, middle finger on the second fret of the A string, and index finger on the second fret of the high E string.
2. Strum the strings: Strum from the low E string down to the high E string. Avoid hitting the low A string.

D Major

1. Put your index finger on the second fret of the string, middle finger on the second fret of the high E string, and ring finger on the third fret of the B string.
2. Strum from the D string down to the high E string. Avoid playing the low E and A strings.

E Minor

1. Put your index finger on the second fret of the A string.
2. Strum from the low E string down to the high E string.

Strumming Techniques

Basic Down-Up Strum

The foundational strumming pattern for every guitarist, the Basic Down-Up Strumming alternates between downward and upward strums. Represented as DUD, this pattern provides a versatile rhythm suitable for various musical styles. Its simplicity makes it an ideal startign point for beginners, forming the basis for more intricate strumming techniques. Mastering this pattern sets the stage for developing a strong sense of rhythm and dynamics in your guitar playing!

,
Island Strum

Imagine the relaxed vibe of a tropical island. Island Strumming, like a gentle ocean breeze, follows the pattern DU UDU. It's a fun and easy way to add a bit of sunshine to your guitar playing. This strumming style is perfect for songs with a laid-back, beachy feel. Just strum away, and you'll feel the island groove in no time!

Rumba Flamenca Strumming

Feel the fiery rhythm of Flamenco with Rumbo Flamenco Strumming. It goes D-UD-U, like the beat of passionate Spanish music. This strumming style adds a touch of excitement and flair to your guitar playing. Try it out, and let your fingers dance to the vibrant sounds of Flamenco!

Rock Ballad Strumming

Picture a slow, powerful rock song. Rock Ballad Strumming, with its steady DDU-DDU pattern, sets the mood for emotional and melodic tunes. It's like a musical hug, creating a feeling of connection and intensity. Perfect for those heartfelt rock ballads that tug at the heartstrings. Give it a strum and feel the emotion!

Tips & Techniques

Improve your playing with our helpful tips and techniques.

Perfecting Your Fretting Hand

Achieve clean and clear notes by focusing on proper finger placement and pressure on the fretboard. Practice scales and exercises to strengthen your fretting hand.

Effective String Bending

Master the art of string bending to add expression to your playing. Control the bend's pitch and release for a dynamic and emotive sound.

Improving Strumming Precision

Enhance your strumming by practicing with a metronome. Focus on maintaining consistent rhythm and strumming patterns for a polished sound.
